Lucas Lejeune is a French multimedia artist based in Strasbourg, who explores the possibilities and limitations of digital technology. He primarily uses text, video, graphic animation, and editing to probe the connections between humans and machines, the natural and the synthetic, and the past and future. His visual work is complemented by a conceptual approach, focused on reflection and reinterpretation of history, culture, and language systems. In doing so, he explores how new technologies enable us to rethink our place in the world and reconnect with our deep humanity. He is also interested in the ethical and social issues raised by the emergence of artificial intelligence, as well as the question of authorship, and hopes to contribute to a deeper reflection on these subjects. His main mediums are video, gifs, and micro-publishing.
